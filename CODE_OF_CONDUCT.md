# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Showing kindness and empathy toward other people
- Being respectful of different opinions and experiences
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback gracefully
- Taking responsibility when we make mistakes, and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the community as a whole

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Using sexualized language or imagery, or making sexual advances of any kind
- Trolling, making insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing someone's private information (such as an address or email) without their permission
- Any other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They will take fair and appropriate action in response to any behavior they find harmful, offensive, or inappropriate.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct. They will explain their reasoning when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, and also applies when someone is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior can be reported by opening an issue or contacting the project maintainer directly on GitHub (@sir-siren).

All reports will be reviewed and investigated. The maintainer will respect the privacy of the person who reports an incident.
